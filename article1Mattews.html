The Economic Divide: How Wage Theft and Inflation Harm America’s Working Class

Illustration by The Hill
Key Terms

Capitalism: An economic system where production and trade is controlled by private owners to turn over a profit, the economic system used in the U.S.

Wage Theft: The illegal practice of employers not paying workers what they are owed, including withholding wages, unpaid overtime, paying less than minimum wage, or denying benefits.

Social Injustice: A situation where unfair practices or policies create inequality and harm certain groups, often based on class, race, or economic status.

Tariffs: Taxes placed on imported goods that the importer has to pay. This usually causes the price of imported items to increase.

Inflation: The increase in prices of goods over time

Economic Inequality: The gap between the rich and the poor in terms of wealth and income distribution within a society.

The Issue With Capitalism

The rich and high class citizens don’t understand the lower classes, which make up the majority of the country. America is built around making the rich richer and the poor poorer. For example, employers will find any way possible to not pay their workers. Some methods they use are paying partial wages, giving unpaid overtime, and flat out failing to pay wages.

Illustration: Getty Images
What this means

Doing this only hurts the employees, because they have to suffer because their boss wanted to save a few dollars, which likely will be next to nothing for them, but devastating for the victim. This is a social injustice because people are being punished for doing their job.

It’s fair to receive a fair day’s pay after a fair day’s work, as this is what allows everyone to eat and sleep safely. Citizens (especially lower-class ones) already struggle to get by when receiving their full paychecks, so getting only part of it or none at all spells doom for them.

Many people live on the streets using whatever money they can get just to eat for the week, and this becomes more widespread if people don’t get their money. Data also shows that this issue is most prominent in construction, food service, hotels, and home healthcare agencies, most of them being easy careers that lower class citizens will work. Employers not paying their workers is increasing poverty rates in the U.S.

Illustration: The Balance by Evan Polenghi
The Rise of Inflation

Inflation is another social injustice that is a big issue in the U.S., especially recently. Prices of goods have been rapidly increasing, but wages have stayed the same. This means people have to pay more money for the same products.

U.S. inflation rate versus wage growth 2020-2024. Published by Statista Research Department, Feb 11, 2025

For rich people, such as president Trump, this isn’t an issue (and it’s also why they’re in favor of his tariffs). Everyone else, however, only suffers as essential goods get more expensive.

For example, egg prices are sky high right now, almost $1 per egg. If it were just a few items it wouldn’t be that much of an issue, but almost everything is really expensive. Eggs, milk, cheese, meat, all the essential food items are only getting more and more expensive. Other prices are rising too, such as the already very expensive cars.

The Federal Reserve Economic Data

If prices continue to rise without salaries also increasing, the average U.S. citizen will have a hard time living a comfortable, let alone stable, life in the United States

Misinformation/lack of information is another issue that is somewhat correlated to inflation. Most U.S. citizens don’t understand what tariffs do. Tariffs place a tax on imported goods, so importing countries increase their prices to make up for it. People don’t realize that tariffs only cause imported goods to be marked up to make up for lost money.
